Red Chile Puree

This spicy puree can be used as an enchilada sauce, a marinade for meat, or used to smother any food your heart desires in a distinctly New Mexican flavor.









Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Yield: 3 Cups


Ingredients:

  • 4 oz. dried chile pods (about 24 pods)
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tsp. salt
  • 2 tsp. vinegar
  • 2 cups of water

Steps:

  1. Remove the stems and seeds from the dried chile pods. Be sure to wash your hands after handling.
  2. Add pods to a medium pot and cover with water. Discard seeds and stems.
  3. Put pot on the stove and bring to a boil. Once chile pods are boiling, reduce heat to a simmer and set a timer for fifteen minutes.
  4. Once fifteen minutes are up, remove the chile pods from the heat and drain them over a colander in the sink.
  5. Put the drained chile pods into a blender, and add the water, garlic, salt, and vinegar. Blend until smooth.
  6. Once blended, pour mixture through a strainer, pushing the puree through the holes with a rubber spatula until only seeds and skins from the chile remain.
  7. Discard the seeds and skins left in the strainer once all the puree has been pushed through.
  8. Use your chile sauce right away, or put into containers to keep in the fridge or freezer.
